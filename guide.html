Rome City Guide



The Eternal City

Rome, the capital city of Italy, is a truly unique and fascinating place to visit. Known as the "Eternal City," Rome has a rich history dating back over two and a half thousand years, and it is one of the birthplaces of Western civilization. With its stunning architecture, beautiful public spaces, and thriving cultural scene, Rome offers visitors a wide range of experiences, from exploring ancient Roman ruins to taking in world-class art and music. Home to a diverse and multicultural population, Rome is a bustling and vibrant city that is constantly changing and evolving, making it a destination that is sure to captivate and inspire.

What to see

The Colosseum
 The Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater in the center of Rome built in 80 AD and is known for hosting gladiatorial contests and other public spectacles.
St. Peter's Basilica
 St. Peter's Basilica is a magnificent Renaissance church located in Vatican City, Rome. It is considered one of the holiest Catholic shrines and is the largest church in the world.
Villa Borghese
 Villa Borghese is a beautiful park in the heart of Rome, known for its vast gardens, museums, and galleries. It features stunning views of the city and hosts a variety of cultural events throughout the year.
Trevi Fountain
 The Trevi Fountain is an iconic Baroque fountain located in the Trevi district of Rome. It is famous for its intricate sculptures and its legend of tossing a coin over your shoulder into the fountain to ensure your return to Rome.
Piazza Navona
 Piazza Navona is a beautiful public square in the heart of Rome, known for its stunning Baroque fountains and architecture. It is a popular gathering place for tourists and locals alike, and features a variety of street performers and artists.
The Pantheon
 The Pantheon is an ancient temple in Rome that is now a church. It is known for its stunning architecture, including the largest unreinforced concrete dome in the world. It also houses the tombs of famous figures such as Raphael and several Italian kings.
Sistine Chapel
 The Sistine Chapel is a famous chapel located in Vatican City known for its intricate frescoes painted by Renaissance artist Michelangelo.
Roman Forum
 The Roman Forum was once the center of ancient Rome and is now a sprawling archaeological site with ruins of ancient government buildings and temples.
Castel Sant Angelo
 Castel Sant'Angelo is a fortress located near the Vatican City, initially built as a mausoleum for the Roman Emperor Hadrian and later used as a papal fortress and castle.
